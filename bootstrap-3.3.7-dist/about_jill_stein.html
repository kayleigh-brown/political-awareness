Jill Stein


Political Overview
 • Stein is a current American activist and politician
 • Green-Rainbow Party's candidate for governor of Massachusetts
 • Ran for Secretary of State, gaining over 350,000 votes

Early Life and Education
 • Born 14 May 1950 in Chicago, Illinois
 • Graduated from Harvard University and Harvard Medical University

After College
 • Protested the "Filthy Five" coal plants in Massachusetts
 • Principal organizer for the Global Climate Convergence for People, Planet, and Peace over Profit
 • Helped close a toxic medical waste incinerator in Lawrence, MA
 • Helped pass the Clean Election Law
 • Founder and past co-chair of the Lexington Board of Selectmen
 • Co-founder of the Massachusetts Coalition for Healthy Communities
 • Received the Clean Water Action's "Not in Anyone's Backyard" and the "Children's Health Hero" Awards
 • Co-author of In Harm's Way: Toxic Threats to Child Development and Environmental Threats to Healthy Aging

Economic Stances
 • Public transportation and critical infrastructure must be invested in
 • Living-wage jobs must be created for every American who needs work
 • The unemployed would have an enforceable right to make the government provide work
 • Enact the Green Deal full employment program to create 20 million green jobs in sustainable energy
 • Replace NAFTA and other free trade agreements which exort American jobs and depress wages
 • Guarantee economic human rights, such as access to food, water, housing, and utilities
 • Free universal child-care
 • Americans must have the right to a living wage job
 • An improved "Medicare for All" must be established
 • Co-pays, premiums, and deuctibles must be eliminated and access to all health care provided
 • Health insurance must be eliminated
 • Allow full access to contraceptive and reproductive care
 • GMO labelling must be required
 • Minimum wage must be set to $15/hour
 • Wall Street, large corporations, and the rich must pay their fair share of taxes
 • Equal pay for equal work
 • Paid sick leave an family leave must be enacted
 • Impose an immediate moratorium on foreclosures and evictions

Educational Stances
 • Tuition-free, world-class public education from pre-school through university must be guaranteed
 • Student debt must be abolished
 • Teacher performance must be assessed through fellow professionals, not high stakes tests
 • Replace Common Core with curriculum developed by educators, with input from parents and communities
 • Students should not be deneid their diplomas based on high stakes tests
 • Art, music, and recreation must be reintroduced to school curriculums
 • America must ensure that children are healthy, nourished, secure, and free from violence
 • The federal funding of public schools must be increased
 • Cut military spending by at least 50% and close the 700+ foreign military bases

Environmental Stances
 • A Green New Deal must be enacted to revive the economy and help turn the tide on climate change
 • By 2030, America should be completely transitioned to clean, renewable energy
 • America must invest in sustainable infrastructure
 • Implement a Just Transition away from fossil fuels
 • Any workers displaced by the Just Transition will receive full income/benefits as they transition to new work
 • Energy must be treated as a human right
 • America needs to build a naitonwide smart electricity grid to store power from clean energy sources
 • Destructive energy extraction, including fracking, tar sands, uranium mines, and offshore drilling, will end
 • A greenhouse gas fee/tax will be implemented to charge polluters for damages incurred
 • Public land, water supplies, biological diversity, parks, and pollinators must be protected
 • Neonicotinoids and others pesticides that threaten bees, butterflies, and other pollinators must be banned
 • A global climate treaty must be induced to limit global warming to no more than 1.5 degrees Celsius
 • Clean air, water, food, and soil must be invested in for everyone. Clean up America

Equality Stances
 • The Equal Rights Amendment will be passed to expand women's rights and reproductive freedom
 • The LGBTQIA+ community must be protected from discrimination
 • Indigenous rights, land, and treaties must be defended
 • Immigrants' rights must be supported, and a welcoming path for citizenship created
 • Deportations and night raids will be halted
 • Border crossings throughout North America must be demilitarized

National Security
 • The failed War on Drugs must be replaced with harm reduction
 • Marijuana must be legalized, and substance abuse treated as a health problem, not a criminal offense
 • Nonviolent drug offenders must be released from prison, and the offenses removed from their records
 • Police brutality, mass incarceration, and institutional racism must be ended. Black Lives Matter
 • The police must be demilitarized, and the use of SWAT teams ended
 • Establish police review boards so that the community control their police, not the other way around

Social Justice Stances
 • The Bill of Rights must be enforced to protect free speech and prevent unwanted search and seizure
 • Unconstitutional surveillence and unwarranted spying must be terinated and Guantanamo closed
 • Abolish the death penalty
 • Repeal the Patriot Act

Foreign Stances
 • End American financial and military support to human rights abusers
 • America must no longer be the world's arm supplier
 • End the use of assassination as an instrument of U.S. foreign policy
 • End the American economic and military intervention in the affairs of sovereign nations
 • American policies supporting the worst tendencies of the Israeli government will be ended
 • Restore the National Guard as the centerpiece of American defense
 • Ban the use of drone aircraft for assassination, bombing, and other offenses
 • End the wars in Iraq and Afghanistan and withdraw troops and military contractors
 • Rejoin the Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty
 • Remove U.S. nuclear weapons in Germany, Belgium, Turkey, Italy, and the Netherlands

Government Reform
 • Include a constitutional amendment to clarify that only humans have constitutional rights, not corporations
 • Eliminate unlimited corporate spending in elections
 • Enforce and expand the constitutional right to vote
 • Abolish the Electoral College and directly elect the President using national popular vote
 • Guarantee equal access to the debates to all ballot-qualified candidates
 • Enact statehood for the District of Columbia to ensure full congressional representation
 • Restore voting rights to offenders, including while in prison
 • Reduce barriers to voting by making Election Day a national holiday
 • Rewrite the tax code to be truly progressive
 • Include tax cuts for working families, the poor and middle class, and higher taxes for the rich
 • Strengthen Medicare and Social Security and remove the cap on social security taxes above a certain income
