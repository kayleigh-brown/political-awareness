Hillary Clinton


Political Overview
 • Secretary of State
 • U.S. Senator: New York
 • First Lady of the United States
 • First Lady of Arkansas

Early Life and Education
 • Born 26 October 1947 in Park Ridge, Illinois
 • Attended Wellesley College after high school
 • Graduated from Yale Law School

After College
 • Worked at the Children's Defense Fund
 • Worked as a lawyer in the congressional committee which investigated President Nixon
 • Taught criminal law, criminal procedure, and trial advocacy in Arkansas
 • Ran the Arkansas legal aid clinic and prison projects
 • Worked as the first female partner at Rose Law Firm
 • Co-founded Arkansas Advocates for Children and Families
 • Chaired the President's Task Force on National Health Care Reform
 • Created the Children's Health Insurance Program
 • Led the U.S. delegation at the U.N. Fourth World Conference on Women in Beijing
 • First U.S. Senator from New York, and eventually re-elected to the Senate with a 67% vote
 • Ran for President in 2008
 • Served as Secretary of State under the Obama administration
